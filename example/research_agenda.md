# Research Agenda (Default)

## Themes

- Prefer problems that develop the current theory toward reusable structural results rather than isolated calculations.

## Valued Problem Types

- Characterizations, equivalences, converse/non-converse results, and sharp boundary statements.
- Existence, uniqueness, rigidity, or classification phenomena that reorganize the theory.

## Anti-Goals

- Cosmetic rewrites, shallow corollaries, and near-duplicates of already visible statements.
- One-off examples whose main value is only local verification.

## Canonical Targets

- Problems whose resolution would noticeably change how the current theory is summarized.

## Soft Constraints

- Stay close to the active theory and its existing notation.
- Prefer statements that are likely to remain valuable even after several more theorems are added.
