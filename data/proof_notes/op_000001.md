# Problem op_000001

## Statement
∀ {α : Type u} [SemigroupLike01 α], ∀ x y z : α, (x * y) * z = x * (y * z)

## Attempt Result
counterexample

## Natural Language Sketch
A 3-element model refutes universal associativity. Define multiplication by the table with rows [0,0,0], [0,1,0], and [0,2,2]. The three axioms of `SemigroupLike01` hold in this model by finite case checking. But for `x = 2`, `y = 1`, `z = 2`, we get `((x * y) * z) = 2` while `x * (y * z) = 0`, so the target statement is false.

## Counterexample Intuition
Take three elements 0, 1, 2. Let 0 absorb everything on the left, let 1 act by sending 1 to 1 and the other inputs to 0, and let 2 send 0 to 0 and both 1 and 2 to 2. This satisfies the given axioms, but with x=2, y=1, z=2 the left-associated product is 2 and the right-associated product is 0.
