# Problem Seeds

This file keeps the report's main research pressures close to problem generation and problem evaluation.

## Generation Anchors

- provability relations among structural rules
- discovery of invertibility in proof search
- rigorous separation of expressive power between systems
- algebraic limits of completeness theorems
- sharp boundaries of decidability and cut-elimination
- structural preservation properties inherent in the mapping from syntactic types to semantic representations via the Curry-Howard correspondence

## Evaluation Checks

- Does this candidate change the summary of the structural theory rather than extend one proof path?
- Is it closer to a structural correspondence, a reducibility claim, an invertibility claim, a separability claim, a completeness limit, or a decidability boundary?
- Is it merely a cosmetic restatement, an unprincipled proliferation of structural rules, a one-off example, or an unproductive negative statement?
- Would solving it help with a canonical target such as completeness, the cut-elimination / decidability boundary, or the syntax-semantics interface?

## Main Theorem Triggers

- algebraic foundations of completeness
- mapping the decidability frontier
- formalizing structural preservation
- integration of hypergraph grammars
